<p><p>The biodegradable nonwovens market includes several key types: </p><p>1. **Drylaid Nonwovens**: Produced by laying fibers in a dry state, these fabrics offer good strength and are often used in applications like wipes and hygiene products.</p><p>2. **Wetlaid Nonwovens**: Created by dispersing fibers in water, this method produces smooth, absorbent materials suitable for filtration and medical applications.</p><p>3. **Spunbond Nonwovens**: Formed by extruding melted polymer onto a conveyor, these nonwovens feature high durability and are commonly used in agricultural and protective clothing applications.</p></p>
