<p><p>The food enzymology market comprises key enzyme types: beta-glucanase, xylanase, pectinase, cellulase, A-amylase, and neutral protease. Beta-glucanase breaks down beta-glucans in grains, enhancing digestibility. Xylanase aids in the degradation of hemicellulose, improving flour quality. Pectinase facilitates fruit juice extraction by breaking down pectin. Cellulase assists in fiber breakdown, enhancing digestion in livestock. A-amylase converts starch into sugars for better fermentation, while neutral protease is vital for protein hydrolysis in food products, optimizing texture and flavor.</p></p>
