<p><ul><li>The Caustic Type</li><li>The Acidic Type</li><li>The Solvent Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The aircraft paint stripper market comprises three primary types: caustic, acidic, and solvent-based strippers. Caustic types use strong alkaline solutions to break down paint layers, offering aggressive removal but requiring careful handling. Acidic types leverage strong acids to dissolve paint, effective on robust layers but potentially damaging to metal surfaces. Solvent types utilize organic solvents to dissolve paint without harsh chemical reactions, providing a balance of efficiency and material compatibility, making them popular for sensitive aircraft surfaces.</p></p>
