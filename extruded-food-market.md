<p><p>The extruded food market comprises two main types: hot extrusion and cold extrusion. Hot extrusion involves the cooking and shaping of ingredients through high temperature and pressure, creating products like pasta and snacks. This method enhances flavor and texture while ensuring food safety. In contrast, cold extrusion processes ingredients at lower temperatures, preserving sensitive nutrients and flavors, making it ideal for products like certain breakfast cereals and pet foods. Both methods cater to diverse consumer needs and preferences.</p></p>
